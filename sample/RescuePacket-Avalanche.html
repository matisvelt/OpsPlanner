Incident Summary (Training):
Title: Avalanche Aftermath: Missing Hiker Recovery (Training Case)
Context: A solo hiker is overdue after an avalanche event in a mountainous region. Last known area is an approximate polygon spanning Sectors A/B/C based on cell ping and witness report. Weather is deteriorating (wind, snowfall) and daylight is limited.
Last Known Area: Polygon across Sectors A/B/C with uncertainty gradient toward Sector C
Time Last Seen: ~4 hours ago (training estimate)
Daylight Window: Approx. 3 hours of usable light remaining (training estimate)
Weather: Wind increasing, snowfall forecast; visibility trending downward.
Comms: Patchy coverage; reliable only in lower elevations and ridgeline gaps.
Medical Risk: Hypothermia risk increases with time; survivability window uncertain.
Note: This draft is for planning and governance only; it does not include procedural rescue actions.

6) COA Builder

COA Summary (Abstract, Non-Tactical):

Ground-first prioritized by probability distribution
Objective: Allocate ground teams to highest-probability sectors while preserving safety gates.
Phases:
- Phase 0: Confirm access, comms, and hazard updates (Timing: 0-30 min)
- Phase 1: Search Sector A then B based on probability weights (Timing: 30-120 min)
- Phase 2: Expand to Sector C/D if gates permit (Timing: 120-240 min)
- Phase 3: Consolidate findings and transition to handover (Timing: 240+ min)
Safety Gates: Avalanche risk gate, Comms blackout gate
Key Assumptions: Ground access remains viable for Sectors A/B
Risks: Time loss if access blocked
Info Needs: Updated probability weights by sector

Safety-first constrained search (minimize responder exposure)
Objective: Limit exposure by restricting search to low-hazard sectors unless gates are cleared.
Phases:
- Phase 0: Confirm gates and hazard updates (Timing: 0-30 min)
- Phase 1: Focus on low-hazard sectors D/E (Timing: 30-120 min)
- Phase 2: Conditional expansion to A/B with safety concurrence (Timing: 120-240 min)
- Phase 3: Reassess and transition to recovery/handover (Timing: 240+ min)

Key Assumptions: Low-hazard sectors still allow meaningful coverage

8) Decision & Safety Gates

- Weather cutoff | Threshold: Wind index >= threshold or visibility <= 2 | Action: Pause/Branch | Owner: Safety Officer
- Avalanche risk gate | Threshold: Hazard index >= 4 in a sector | Action: Stop/Go by sector | Owner: Safety Officer
- Comms blackout gate | Threshold: No check-in for 30 min | Action: Pause and re-establish comms | Owner: Comms & Tracking
- Medical threshold gate | Threshold: Survivability window < 2 hours | Action: Reprioritize and compress timeline | Owner: Medical Lead
Stop/Go Criteria: Stop or branch if any gate triggers beyond threshold; continue only with Safety Officer concurrence.

Rescue Cell will conduct coordinated search and recovery of missing hiker within Approx. 3 hours of usable light remaining (training estimate) across A, B, C, D, E under Aviation restricted above wind index threshold; whiteout risk. in order to achieve the end state: locate, stabilize, and handover.
